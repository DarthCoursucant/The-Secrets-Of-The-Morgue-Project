The Full Story Of Flight 105 UFO Sighting

The Flight 105 UFO sighting, also known as the "Trans-Canada Airlines Flight 105 Incident," is one of the most notable UFO encounters involving a commercial aircraft and its passengers. It occurred on December 12, 1955, when a Trans-Canada Airlines (TCA) flight was en route from Montreal to Toronto. The incident has garnered considerable attention due to the credible witnesses involved and the unusual nature of the sighting.

Background

On December 12, 1955, Trans-Canada Airlines Flight 105 was a routine passenger flight departing from Montreal. The aircraft, a Douglas DC-4, was carrying 38 passengers and crew members. The flight was piloted by Captain R. J. "Bob" McGowan, who had extensive experience as a commercial pilot.

As the flight ascended to cruising altitude, it entered a clear and calm night sky, providing excellent visibility. The crew and passengers were settling in for the journey when an extraordinary event began to unfold.

The Sighting

Approximately 20 minutes into the flight, while cruising at an altitude of about 9,000 feet, the flight crew noticed a bright light in the sky. Initially, they thought it could be another aircraft or a celestial object. However, as they observed the light more closely, it became apparent that it was moving in a way that was unlike any conventional aircraft.

Captain McGowan, along with his co-pilot, First Officer H. F. "Hank" McGowan, began to track the light. It was described as a luminous object, glowing brightly and moving erratically against the backdrop of the night sky. The object was reported to have a reddish hue and appeared to be changing colors as it moved.

Communication with Air Traffic Control

Concerned about the unidentified object, the flight crew contacted Air Traffic Control to report their sighting. They described the light and its movements, seeking clarification on whether they were in the vicinity of any other aircraft. Air Traffic Control confirmed that there were no other flights in the area and advised the crew to continue monitoring the object.

As the crew continued to observe the light, it became apparent that it was now approaching the aircraft. The object was moving at a high rate of speed, and the pilots were increasingly alarmed by its proximity. Captain McGowan described the object as being “like a bright star,” but its movements were erratic and unpredictable.

Close Encounter

As Flight 105 continued its flight, the unidentified object began to maneuver around the aircraft. Crew members reported that the object would suddenly accelerate, then slow down, and even hover in place above them. The behavior of the object was unlike anything they had ever encountered.

Witnesses aboard the flight began to panic as the object continued to draw closer. Some passengers reported seeing a distinct shape, describing the object as disc-like or oval. Others claimed to see a series of lights flashing on and off along the craft’s edge.

The flight crew remained calm and focused on the safety of their passengers, trying to assess the situation. They made several maneuvers to ensure that they maintained a safe distance from the object while continuing to communicate with Air Traffic Control.

Air Traffic Control Response

As the incident unfolded, Air Traffic Control was monitoring the situation closely. They contacted nearby military bases to determine if any aircraft were conducting exercises in the area. Their inquiries revealed that no military aircraft were in the vicinity, heightening the mystery surrounding the sighting.

The crew of Flight 105 continued to observe the object, which eventually began to move away from them. After approximately 10 minutes of close proximity, the unidentified object accelerated rapidly and disappeared from view, leaving the pilots and passengers in a state of disbelief.

Aftermath and Investigation

Upon landing in Toronto, the flight crew reported the incident to the airline and local authorities. The sighting quickly gained media attention, with newspapers reporting on the strange encounter. The incident was investigated by Trans-Canada Airlines and, subsequently, by the Royal Canadian Air Force (RCAF), which had been alerted to the sighting.

The RCAF conducted interviews with the flight crew and passengers, gathering details about the encounter. Despite the thorough investigation, no definitive explanation was provided for the sighting. The lack of physical evidence, combined with the unusual nature of the object, left many questions unanswered.

Legacy

The Flight 105 UFO sighting is often cited as one of the most credible encounters involving commercial aviation. The incident demonstrated that pilots and crew members could experience sightings of unidentified flying objects, raising awareness of the phenomenon and its potential implications for aviation safety.

Over the years, the incident has been referenced in books, documentaries, and discussions about UFO phenomena and government investigations. It remains a significant case in the study of UFOs, illustrating the complexities of human experiences with the unknown.

Conclusion

The Flight 105 UFO sighting of December 12, 1955, stands as a compelling chapter in the history of unidentified flying objects. The combination of credible witnesses, including experienced pilots and passengers, and the unusual nature of the encounter contribute to its lasting impact in UFO lore. While the mystery surrounding the sighting remains unresolved, it serves as a reminder of humanity's ongoing quest to understand the unknown and the possibility of life beyond our planet.
